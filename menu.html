Step 2: Menu

 
Food
  • Are guests contributing menu items?
    Potluck? or Assignments?
  • Write down and save menu in a safe place!
  • Budget (Cater, Make yourself, Combination)
  • Effort involved (again Cater, Make yourself, Combination)
  • Schedule extra time to grocery shop.

To consider:

  • Is a meal being served?
  • Appetizers, snacks (often overlooked).
  • What serving dishes do you have?
Beverages
  • This is the item that most guests will contribute (and usually alcohol - assuming an adult party).
  • However, you will still need to have plenty of beverages on hand.
    • Water (bottled, pitcher, dispenser) should be readily available.
    • A bowl of punch is a great idea and can easily be themed.
    • Soft drinks - soda, juice, lemonade, gatorade.
    • Alcoholic beverages - beer, wine & mixed drinks.

To consider:

  • Have someone serve as or hire a bartender?
  • Have guests bring their own?
  • If at a venue, it's ok to have a cash bar OR a partially open bar for beer, wine, soda - cash for everything else.
  • At home, make sure alcohol is separate from other drinks - especially if kids / teens will be present.
  • What's the weather like? Guests will drink more (and eat less) on very warm days.
